# Contributing

Thank you for your interest in contributing to the Request Governance Platform (RGP).

Before any outside contribution is accepted, contributors must agree to the repository's contributor licensing policy.

## Contribution Prerequisite

By submitting a pull request, patch, documentation change, issue-attached code sample, or any other contribution intended for inclusion in this repository, you represent that you have the legal right to submit that contribution and you agree to the terms in:

- [CLA.md](./CLA.md)

If you do not agree to those terms, do not submit a contribution for inclusion in this repository.

## Why This Policy Exists

RGP is published under noncommercial public licenses:

- code under PolyForm Noncommercial 1.0.0
- documentation and other non-software content under CC BY-NC 4.0

Paul Bernard, as project maintainer, intends to retain the exclusive ability to:

- offer commercial exceptions
- offer separate commercial licenses
- dual-license or otherwise relicense the project in the future

To preserve that flexibility, contributions are accepted only under terms that preserve Paul Bernard's right to grant separate commercial rights.

## Practical Contribution Rules

- All commercial-use rights remain reserved except where separately granted in writing by Paul Bernard or his authorized designee.
- Commercial exceptions are granted only by Paul Bernard or his authorized designee.
- Contributions are accepted only under the CLA terms referenced above.
- Do not submit code, content, or assets that you do not have the right to license under those terms.

## Development Expectations

Contributors should also:

- follow the constitution, requirements, and build-pack model
- preserve the canonical vocabulary and domain model
- keep UX aligned with the table-first IA and design system
- add or update tests when behavior changes
- avoid introducing undocumented architectural drift

## Questions

If you want to contribute but need different terms, or if you are seeking commercial-use rights, contact Paul Bernard before contributing.
